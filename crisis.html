The Civil War: Five Ropes, Six Options

This isn't about opinions. The code shows: Satoshi designed a 40-byte data ceiling. SegWit created a 3MB data lane with 75% discount. Taproot removed the last size constraint. 100,000x increase in per-block data capacity, at 75% discount.

  1. Code archaeology — documenting the divergence with receipts

  3. Anchoring protocol standard (bridge between Bitcoin txs and off-chain data)
  4. Education — website, parable, accessible explanations
Tier 2: Requires Adoption, No Consensus Change
  1. Knots-style filtering in Core (PR to Bitcoin Core, policy only)
  2. Creator tools on anchoring protocol
  3. L3 prototype (proof of concept for data layer)
Tier 3: Requires Consensus
  1. BIP for MAX_TAPSCRIPT_SIZE (restore script size limit)
  2. BIP for witness discount adjustment (reduce discount for non-signature data)
  3. BIP for unknown leaf version rejection
The strategy: Build credibility with Tier 1 → Gain influence with Tier 2 → Push Tier 3 from position of strength.

Inscriptions Might Be Keeping the Lights On

Strip away the moral argument. Look at it cold: miner revenue = subsidy + fees. Subsidy is shrinking on a fixed schedule. Fee revenue from normal transactions is inconsistent. Inscription fees have been a meaningful chunk of fee revenue since 2023. Lightning moves transactions off L1, reducing fee pressure.

If inscriptions vanished tomorrow—if you got everything the sound money camp wants—what replaces that fee revenue?

Nothing. Not yet. Nobody's designed it.

Three Possible Worlds

World A: Inscriptions Persist

Miners get fee revenue. Nodes bear storage costs. Decentralization degrades slowly. Bitcoin survives but drifts from sound money.

World B: Inscriptions Die

Miners lose significant fee revenue. Hash rate drops. Network security degrades. 51% attack cost decreases. Bitcoin becomes vulnerable to state-level attack. World B is worse than World A.

World C: Something Replaces Both

The only world where Bitcoin survives as sound money. Nobody has designed this yet. The clock is running.

The signal is: the anchor is almost gone, and there is no plan. Everything else—the inscriptions, the L3, the filtering, the civil war—is noise around the signal. Write the plan.

Future Threats: Nation States & AI Agents

The inscription civil war is a dress rehearsal for the real conflict coming in 2032–2036, when the anchor is a pebble and two new players arrive that nobody wrote rules for.

Nation-State Actors

  • Digital eminent domain — state-owned mining, regulatory capture of pools
  • Selective reorgs — rewrite last 6 blocks when sanctioned entities move funds
  • Transaction censorship — your Bitcoin exists, it just can't move without approval
  • As subsidy → 0, attack cost drops. A $3B spend is a rounding error in a defense budget.

AI Agents

  • Fill every cheap byte — witness data at 75% discount, operational data not art
  • Out-compete humans — millisecond-speed fee optimization
  • Extract MEV — front-running, sandwich attacks at machine speed
  • No morals, no values, just one question: is this profitable?
